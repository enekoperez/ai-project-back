# Tennis

Tennis is a racket sport played either between two players (singles) or between two pairs of players (doubles). The objective is to hit the ball over the net and into the opponent's court in a way that the opponent cannot return it. Tennis is played on a variety of surfaces including grass, clay, and hard court.

## Basic Rules

A tennis match is divided into sets, and each set is divided into games. To win a game, a player must score at least four points and lead by at least two. Points are counted as 15, 30, 40, and game. When both players reach 40, the score is called deuce, and one player must win two consecutive points to take the game.

To win a set, a player must win at least six games and lead by at least two. If the set reaches six games all, a tiebreak is usually played. In most professional matches, men play best of five sets and women play best of three sets.

The serve begins each point. The server must stand behind the baseline and hit the ball diagonally into the opponent's service box. Each player gets two serve attempts per point. A serve that touches the net and lands in the correct service box is called a let and is replayed. A serve that misses the service box is called a fault. Two faults in a row result in a double fault, and the server loses the point.

## Court And Equipment

A tennis court is rectangular with a net across the middle. The court is divided by lines marking the service boxes, the baseline, and the sidelines. In singles, the inner sidelines are used. In doubles, the outer sidelines are used, making the court wider.

Players use a racket to hit a felt-covered rubber ball. Players typically wear light athletic clothing and tennis shoes designed for the court surface.

## Useful RAG Facts

- Number of players per side: 1 in singles, 2 in doubles.
- Points in a game: 15, 30, 40, game.
- Games needed to win a set: at least 6, with a 2-game lead.
- Tiebreak: played when a set reaches 6-6.
- Serve attempts per point: 2.
- Double fault: losing a point by missing both serve attempts.
- Deuce: score of 40-40, requiring 2 consecutive points to win the game.
- Common match formats: best of 3 sets or best of 5 sets.
- Playing surfaces: grass, clay, hard court.
