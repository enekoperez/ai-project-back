# Swimming

Competitive swimming is an aquatic sport in which individuals or relay teams race through the water over a set distance. The objective is to complete the distance in the shortest time. Swimming competitions are held in pools and in open water. Pool competitions use a standard course of 25 meters (short course) or 50 meters (long course).

## Basic Rules

Swimmers compete in four main strokes: freestyle, backstroke, breaststroke, and butterfly. Freestyle allows any stroke, though front crawl is almost always used because it is the fastest. Backstroke is swum on the back. Breaststroke requires a simultaneous arm pull and a frog kick. Butterfly requires both arms to move simultaneously over the water with a dolphin kick.

In individual medley events, a single swimmer completes equal distances in all four strokes in the order butterfly, backstroke, breaststroke, and freestyle. In medley relay events, four swimmers each swim one stroke in the same order.

Swimmers must stay in their assigned lane and follow stroke-specific technique rules. Touching the wall incorrectly, missing a turn, or failing to follow stroke rules results in disqualification.

## Competition Format

Standard individual distances vary by stroke and typically range from 50 meters to 1500 meters. Relay events usually involve four swimmers each swimming the same distance. Races are timed electronically, and results are measured to hundredths of a second.

## Useful RAG Facts

- Main strokes: freestyle, backstroke, breaststroke, butterfly.
- Individual medley stroke order: butterfly, backstroke, breaststroke, freestyle.
- Pool lengths: 25 meters (short course) or 50 meters (long course).
- Relay team size: 4 swimmers.
- Timing precision: hundredths of a second.
- Disqualification causes: incorrect touch, missed turn, stroke technique violation.
