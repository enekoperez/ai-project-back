# Cricket

Cricket is a bat-and-ball sport played between two teams of eleven players on a large oval field. The objective is to score more runs than the opposing team. Cricket is especially popular in England, Australia, India, Pakistan, the West Indies, and southern Africa. Matches can last from a few hours to five days depending on the format.

## Basic Rules

A cricket match is organized around innings. In each innings, one team bats and the other fields. The batting team sends two players to the pitch at a time. The fielding team's bowler delivers the ball toward the wicket, which the batter defends. A run is scored when both batters successfully run to exchange ends, or when the batter hits the ball to the boundary.

Each over consists of six deliveries bowled by the same bowler. A different bowler bowls each over. A batter is dismissed (out) in several ways: bowled (ball hits the wicket), caught (fielder catches the ball before it bounces), leg before wicket (ball would have hit the wicket but struck the batter's leg first), run out (batter fails to reach the crease before a fielder breaks the wicket), and others.

When ten of the eleven batters are dismissed, the innings ends. The eleventh player cannot bat alone, so only ten dismissals are needed to end an innings.

## Formats

Cricket is played in several formats. Test cricket is the longest format, lasting up to five days with each team batting twice. One Day Internationals (ODIs) limit each team to 50 overs per innings. Twenty20 (T20) matches limit each team to 20 overs per innings and are the shortest and fastest format.

## Field And Equipment

The pitch is a rectangular strip at the center of the oval field. A wicket consisting of three upright stumps topped by two bails stands at each end of the pitch. Batters use a flat-faced bat with a ridge on the back. The ball is hard, made of cork and leather, and is red in traditional formats or white in limited-overs formats.

## Useful RAG Facts

- Number of players per team: 11.
- Deliveries per over: 6.
- Dismissals to end an innings: 10.
- Run scored by: both batters exchanging ends, or ball reaching the boundary.
- Boundary values: 4 runs if ball reaches the boundary rolling; 6 runs if ball clears the boundary without bouncing.
- Test match length: up to 5 days, each team bats twice.
- ODI format: 50 overs per team per innings.
- T20 format: 20 overs per team per innings.
- Common dismissals: bowled, caught, leg before wicket, run out.
