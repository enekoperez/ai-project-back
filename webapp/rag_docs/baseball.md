# Baseball

Baseball is a bat-and-ball sport played between two teams of nine players on a diamond-shaped field. The objective is to score runs by hitting a pitched ball and advancing around four bases to reach home plate. Baseball is widely played in North America, Latin America, and East Asia.

## Basic Rules

Each game is divided into nine innings. In each inning, both teams take a turn batting (offense) and fielding (defense). The batting team sends one player at a time to home plate to face the pitcher. The pitcher throws the ball toward the batter, who tries to hit it. A strike is called when a pitch crosses the strike zone without being hit, or when the batter swings and misses. Three strikes result in a strikeout and the batter is out. Four balls, which are pitches outside the strike zone that the batter does not swing at, result in a walk, allowing the batter to advance to first base.

The batting team scores a run when a player successfully advances around all four bases and reaches home plate. A home run is scored when the batter hits the ball out of the playing field in fair territory, allowing them and any runners on base to score automatically.

The fielding team records outs to end the batting half of an inning. Three outs end the inning. Common ways to record an out include a strikeout, a fly out (fielder catches a hit ball before it touches the ground), or a ground out (fielder throws the ball to first base before the batter arrives).

## Field And Equipment

The baseball field has four bases arranged in a diamond: home plate, first base, second base, and third base. The pitcher throws from the pitcher's mound at the center of the diamond. The outfield extends beyond the infield. Players use a rounded wooden or metal bat and a small, hard leather ball. Fielders use gloves to catch the ball.

## Useful RAG Facts

- Number of players per team: 9.
- Number of innings per game: 9.
- Outs needed to end a batting half-inning: 3.
- Strikes to record a strikeout: 3.
- Balls to award a walk: 4.
- Run scored by: advancing through all four bases back to home plate.
- Home run: ball hit out of the field in fair territory, all runners score.
- Key positions: pitcher, catcher, first baseman, second baseman, third baseman, shortstop, and three outfielders.
