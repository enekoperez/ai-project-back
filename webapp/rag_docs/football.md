# Football

Football, also called soccer in some countries, is a team sport played between two teams of eleven players. The main objective is to score goals by moving the ball into the opponent's net. It is one of the most widely played and watched sports in the world, with professional leagues, international tournaments, youth academies, amateur clubs, and school teams in many countries.

## Basic Objective

The objective of football is simple: score more goals than the opposing team. A goal is scored when the whole ball crosses the goal line between the goalposts and beneath the crossbar, as long as the attacking team has not broken a rule during the play.

Teams try to move the ball up the field through passing, dribbling, movement without the ball, and tactical positioning. A team in possession of the ball is usually attacking, while the team without the ball is usually defending. Possession can change many times during a match.

## Players And Positions

A standard football team has eleven players on the field. These players usually include one goalkeeper and ten outfield players. Outfield players are commonly grouped into defenders, midfielders, and forwards.

The goalkeeper is responsible for protecting the goal. Goalkeepers are the only players who can use their hands and arms to control the ball during normal play, but only inside their own penalty area. Outside the penalty area, the goalkeeper follows the same handball rules as every other player.

Defenders usually play closer to their own goal. Their main job is to stop attacks, block shots, mark opposing players, and help the goalkeeper prevent goals. Common defensive roles include center-back, full-back, and wing-back.

Midfielders usually play between the defenders and forwards. They often connect the team by receiving passes, controlling tempo, winning loose balls, and creating chances. Some midfielders focus on defending, some focus on passing, and some focus on attacking.

Forwards usually play closest to the opponent's goal. Their main job is to create and score goals. Common attacking roles include striker, winger, and center-forward.

## Match Duration

A standard football match lasts 90 minutes. The match is divided into two halves of 45 minutes. There is usually a halftime break between the halves. The referee may add stoppage time at the end of each half to account for time lost because of injuries, substitutions, goal celebrations, disciplinary incidents, or other delays.

In some knockout competitions, if the score is tied after 90 minutes, the match may go to extra time. Extra time usually consists of two halves of 15 minutes. If the match is still tied after extra time, a penalty shootout may be used to decide the winner.

## Field And Equipment

Football is played on a rectangular field, also called a pitch. The field may use natural grass or artificial turf. Each end of the field has a goal. The goal consists of two vertical goalposts and a horizontal crossbar.

Important field markings include the halfway line, the center circle, the penalty areas, the goal areas, the corner arcs, and the touchlines. The touchlines run along the sides of the field, while the goal lines run across the ends.

The ball is spherical and usually made from synthetic leather or similar materials. Players wear shirts, shorts, socks, shin guards, and football boots. Goalkeepers often wear gloves to help catch or parry shots.

## Core Rules

Players mostly use their feet to pass, dribble, and shoot the ball. They can also use other parts of the body, such as the head, chest, or thigh. Outfield players cannot deliberately handle the ball with their hands or arms.

A throw-in is awarded when the ball completely crosses a touchline. The team that did not touch the ball last takes the throw-in. The player taking the throw-in must throw the ball from behind and over the head while keeping both feet near the ground.

A corner kick is awarded when the defending team last touches the ball before it crosses its own goal line without a goal being scored. A goal kick is awarded when the attacking team last touches the ball before it crosses the defending team's goal line without a goal being scored.

A free kick may be awarded after a foul or other rule violation. Free kicks can be direct or indirect. A direct free kick can be kicked straight into the opponent's goal. An indirect free kick must touch another player before a goal can be scored.

A penalty kick is awarded for certain fouls committed by the defending team inside its own penalty area. During a penalty kick, one attacking player shoots from the penalty spot against the goalkeeper.

## Fouls And Discipline

Common fouls include tripping, pushing, holding, charging unfairly, reckless tackles, and deliberate handball. The referee decides when a foul has occurred and what restart should be used.

Referees can use yellow cards and red cards to discipline players. A yellow card is a caution. A red card sends a player off the field, and that player's team must continue with fewer players. A player who receives two yellow cards in the same match is usually shown a red card.

Referees are supported by assistant referees, who help judge offside, throw-ins, fouls near the touchline, and whether the ball has crossed a boundary. In many professional competitions, video assistant referee technology may also be used to review major incidents.

## Offside

Offside is one of the most important and misunderstood football rules. In simple terms, an attacking player may be in an offside position if they are nearer to the opponent's goal line than both the ball and the second-last opponent when a teammate plays the ball to them.

Being in an offside position is not always an offense. The player must become involved in active play, such as receiving the ball, interfering with an opponent, or gaining an advantage from the position. Offside does not apply directly from a throw-in, goal kick, or corner kick.

## Tactics And Formations

Football teams use formations to organize players on the field. A formation describes the general shape of the team, such as 4-4-2, 4-3-3, or 3-5-2. The numbers usually refer to defenders, midfielders, and forwards, excluding the goalkeeper.

A 4-4-2 formation has four defenders, four midfielders, and two forwards. A 4-3-3 formation has four defenders, three midfielders, and three forwards. A 3-5-2 formation has three defenders, five midfielders, and two forwards.

Tactics can change during a match. A team that is winning may defend deeper and protect the lead. A team that is losing may take more risks, push players forward, and press aggressively to recover the ball quickly.

## Competitions

Football is played in domestic leagues, domestic cups, continental competitions, and international tournaments. In a league, teams usually play many matches across a season and earn points for wins and draws. A common points system gives three points for a win, one point for a draw, and zero points for a loss.

International football includes national teams representing countries. The FIFA World Cup is one of the most famous international football tournaments. It is traditionally held every four years and features national teams from around the world.

Club football includes teams that represent cities, regions, institutions, or private organizations. Famous club competitions include national leagues and continental tournaments. Clubs often sign players through transfers and develop younger players through academies.
