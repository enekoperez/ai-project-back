# Ice Hockey

Ice hockey is a fast contact sport played on an ice rink between two teams of six players each. Players skate and use sticks to shoot a rubber puck into the opponent's goal. It is one of the most popular sports in Canada, the United States, Russia, and several European countries.

## Basic Rules

Each team has six players on the ice at a time: one goaltender and five skaters. The skaters are usually arranged as two defensemen and three forwards (center and two wingers). Teams can make substitutions freely during play without stopping the game.

A standard game consists of three periods of twenty minutes each, with breaks between periods. The team that scores the most goals wins. If the score is tied at the end of regulation, many competitions proceed to overtime or a shootout to determine the winner.

Players advance the puck by skating with it, passing it to teammates, or shooting it. Passing the puck forward is allowed, unlike in some other sports. Players may not use their hands to catch or carry the puck, though they may bat it down with an open hand.

## Penalties And Stoppages

Infractions such as tripping, slashing, hooking, or charging result in penalties. A penalized player must spend time in the penalty box, leaving their team short-handed. Common penalties last two minutes (minor) or five minutes (major). When a team is short-handed and the opposing team has a power play, they have a numerical advantage on the ice.

Two important rules are icing and offside. Icing is called when a player shoots the puck from behind the center line across the opponent's goal line without the puck touching another player. Offside is called when an attacking player enters the offensive zone before the puck.

## Useful RAG Facts

- Number of players per team on ice: 6 (1 goaltender, 5 skaters).
- Number of periods: 3, each lasting 20 minutes.
- Main objective: shoot the puck into the opponent's goal.
- Forward pass: allowed.
- Penalty duration: 2 minutes (minor), 5 minutes (major).
- Power play: team advantage when opposing player is in the penalty box.
- Icing: illegal shot from behind center line that crosses the opponent's goal line untouched.
- Offside: attacker enters offensive zone before the puck.
