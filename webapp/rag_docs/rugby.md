# Rugby

Rugby union is a contact team sport played between two teams of fifteen players on a rectangular grass field. The objective is to score more points than the opposing team by carrying, passing, or kicking an oval ball to score tries or goals. Rugby union is distinct from rugby league, which uses thirteen players per side and different rules.

## Basic Rules

Players advance the ball by running with it or kicking it. The ball can only be passed laterally or backward to a teammate, never forward. When a player is tackled and brought to the ground, they must release the ball immediately. Play continues through contests called rucks and mauls.

The main ways to score are the try, the conversion, the penalty kick, and the drop goal. A try is scored by grounding the ball on or over the opponent's try line and is worth five points. After a try, the scoring team gets a conversion kick worth two additional points. A successful penalty kick is worth three points. A drop goal, kicked from open play, is also worth three points.

A standard match lasts 80 minutes, divided into two halves of 40 minutes. The referee controls the match and is assisted by two assistant referees and a television match official in professional games.

## Offside And Set Pieces

Offside rules apply throughout the game, particularly around rucks, mauls, and scrums. A scrum is a set piece used to restart play after certain stoppages. Eight players from each team bind together and push against each other, with the ball fed into the tunnel between them. A lineout restarts play when the ball goes into touch (out of bounds along the sideline). Players from both teams line up and contest for the ball thrown in from the sideline.

## Useful RAG Facts

- Number of players per team: 15 (rugby union); 13 (rugby league).
- Match duration: 80 minutes (two halves of 40 minutes).
- Try value: 5 points.
- Conversion kick (after a try): 2 points.
- Penalty kick: 3 points.
- Drop goal: 3 points.
- Ball passing rule: lateral or backward only, never forward.
- Key set pieces: scrum and lineout.
- Tackle rule: player must release the ball immediately after being tackled.
