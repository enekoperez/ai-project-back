# Volleyball

Volleyball is a team sport played between two teams of six players separated by a net. The objective is to send the ball over the net and ground it on the opponent's side of the court while preventing the opponent from doing the same. It is played both indoors and on sand (beach volleyball, which uses two players per side).

## Basic Rules

Each team is allowed a maximum of three touches to return the ball over the net. The same player cannot touch the ball twice in a row. A rally begins with a serve from behind the end line. The serving team earns a point when the rally ends in their favor. A point is also awarded when the opposing team commits a fault such as hitting the ball out of bounds, failing to return it over the net, or touching it more than three times.

Sets are played to 25 points, and a team must lead by at least 2 points to win a set. A match is usually best of five sets. The deciding fifth set, if needed, is played to 15 points. Teams switch sides after each set, and they rotate player positions each time they gain the serve.

Players use specific positions on the court: setter, outside hitter, middle blocker, opposite hitter, and libero. The libero is a defensive specialist who wears a different colored jersey and cannot serve or attack the ball above the net height.

## Court And Equipment

An indoor volleyball court is rectangular with a net stretched across the center. The net height differs for men and women in competition. The ball is light, round, and slightly larger than a basketball.

## Useful RAG Facts

- Number of players per team: 6 (indoor); 2 (beach volleyball).
- Maximum touches per team per rally: 3.
- Points to win a set: 25 (with a 2-point lead); 15 in the deciding set.
- Match format: best of 5 sets.
- Serving: one player serves until a fault occurs and the opposing team wins a rally.
- Libero: defensive specialist, different jersey, cannot attack above net height.
- Rotation: teams rotate positions each time they win back the serve.
